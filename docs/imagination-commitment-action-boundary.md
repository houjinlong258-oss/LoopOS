# Imagination, Commitment, and Action Boundary

LoopOS has three boundaries.

## Imagination Sandbox

Free thinking, brainstorms, alternatives, design hypotheses, and exploration.
No side effects. No policy block. No syscall. No file or network mutation.

## Commitment Boundary

The point where an idea becomes a plan, ACI request, patch proposal, or
syscall request. Governance begins here because authority changes here.

## Action Boundary

Real execution: files, shell, network, databases, release, external services,
permission escalation, and real cost. This boundary must go through Policy OS,
Syscall Router, approval when required, and Trace.

Rules:

- Policy gates actions, not ideas.
- Syscall gates side effects, not reasoning.
- Mad Dog attacks claims, not imagination.
